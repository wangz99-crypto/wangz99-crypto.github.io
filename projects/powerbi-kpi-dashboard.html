📊 Corporate KPI Dashboard — GD-OTS

Seven-page Power BI dashboard built for General Dynamics Ordnance and Tactical Systems as a University of Dayton BAN 710 Business Analytics Capstone (Spring 2026). Delivers 30-second KPI attribution across 24 manufacturing sites using weighted roll-ups, leave-one-out impact analysis, and 20+ validated DAX measures.

Power BI DAX SPQDC Framework Weighted Roll-Up Waterfall Analysis Executive Reporting Data Modeling Power Query

1) Background & Problem

General Dynamics Ordnance and Tactical Systems (GD-OTS) operates 24 manufacturing sites organized into a four-level hierarchy — sites → subgroups → Strategic Business Units → corporate. Monthly performance reviews track five KPI categories collectively known as SPQDC: Safety, People, Quality, Delivery, and Cost.

Prior to this project, all SPQDC reporting was produced manually in Excel. Performance was shown as flat company-level percentages with no visibility into which site was responsible for a KPI miss or by how much. Root-causing a miss required pulling and reconciling multiple spreadsheets — a process that consumed significant time during monthly reviews.

The requirement set at kickoff was precise: identify which site is driving a KPI miss and by how much, in under 30 seconds, without manual spreadsheet work.

2) Methodology

2.1 Weighted Roll-Up (No Averaging of Averages)

All company-level and SBU-level figures aggregate raw numerators and denominators across sites first, then compute one weighted result. Simple percentage averaging gives equal weight to a site handling 100 lines and one handling 10,000 — the weighted approach ensures that site scale is correctly reflected at every organizational level. This distinction changed which sites appeared as highest-impact contributors during development validation.

2.2 Leave-One-Out Site Impact Logic

The KPI Performance Drivers page computes each site's marginal impact on the company total. For every site, the dashboard calculates the full company KPI, then removes that site's contribution using ALLEXCEPT / REMOVEFILTERS in DAX, and returns the difference. Green bars in the waterfall chart represent sites that move the KPI toward target; red bars pull it away. This directly answers the attribution question without any manual consolidation.

2.3 Validation Framework

January 2025 was selected as the baseline validation month — the first period with complete data across all 24 sites. Every DAX measure was validated against the client's existing Excel figures at the site, subgroup, SBU, and corporate levels. The final QA check produced zero calculation errors before the dashboard was delivered.

2.4 Data Governance

  • Placeholder values (–1 for not-applicable) converted to null in Power Query before any measure runs
  • Site names standardized across all source files to prevent silent join failures
  • All model relationships set to single-direction one-to-many (bidirectional filtering removed after it produced wrong values at SBU-level filter contexts)
  • GD-OTS data team can load new monthly files independently — no preprocessing or analyst involvement required

3) Dashboard Architecture

The model follows a star schema: a central KPI fact table joined to dimension tables for sites, months, targets, and KPI categories. Source data is loaded from five Excel files via six Power Query queries that handle all cleaning before data enters the model.

All seven pages share a cascading filter panel — Year, Month, SBU, Group, Site — that updates every visual simultaneously. Selecting an SBU automatically limits the available Group and Site options to that unit's hierarchy.

Seven Dashboard Pages

  • Page 1 — Executive KPI Overview: Five scorecards (Safety, People, Quality, Delivery, Cost) with sub-metric toggles, five monthly trend lines, and a site-level hit/miss heatmap across all 24 sites and all five KPI categories.
  • Page 2 — KPI Performance Drivers: Leave-one-out waterfall + ranked bar chart. Select any SPQDC category; all visuals update to show each site's marginal impact on that KPI.
  • Pages 3–7 — Individual KPI Drill-Downs: One page per SPQDC category. Each shows a scorecard vs. target, historical trend chart, monthly trend vs. target line, and a site ranking chart.

All seven pages of the delivered dashboard, covering the executive overview, performance driver analysis, and individual drill-downs for each SPQDC category.

Page 1 — Executive KPI Overview

Five KPI scorecards with sub-metric toggles, monthly trend lines, and a hit/miss heatmap across all 24 sites for the selected period.

Page 2 — KPI Performance Drivers

Leave-one-out waterfall and ranked site bar chart. Answers "which site is driving this KPI miss and by how much" in under 30 seconds for any selected SPQDC category.

Page 3 — Safety Drill-Down

Recordable Injury Rate (injuries × 200,000 / exposure hours) vs. target, with historical trend and site ranking by highest injury rate.

Page 4 — People Drill-Down

Monthly employee retention rate with Hourly / Salaried toggle, historical trend, and site ranking. Roll-ups weighted by headcount, not averaged.

Page 5 — Quality Drill-Down

Scrap $ and Scrap % of Sales toggle. Monthly scrap trend vs. target and top sites by scrap dollar value — directly actionable cost-of-waste visibility.

Page 6 — Delivery Drill-Down

On-Time Delivery % and Lines Missed per 100 toggle. OTD trend vs. target and top sites by OTD — volume-weighted so a large-site miss isn't masked by percentage alone.

Page 7 — Cost Drill-Down

Labor Efficiency % (earned hours / actual hours) vs. target, with historical trend and top sites by labor efficiency. Hours Over per 100 toggle available.
